Module One: Phishing Explained


Phishing is a type of social engineering attack often used to steal user data, including login credentials and credit card numbers.

What is Phishing?

Phishing is called a social engineering attack because it involves social manipulation. Scammers often rely on emotions such as fear, anxiety or confusion to trick people into sharing confidential data.

The name 'phishing' is in reference to the activity of fishing.

Similarly to fishing, phishing attacks are often broad attacks that rely on one or two people taking the 'bait'. Phishing emails or messages are often sent to many people in the hopes that one or two may share confidential and private information.

Often, all it takes is one person to share information that they shouldn't in order to jeprodise an entire company or organisation.


Phishing, Smishing, Vishing

Phishing, smishing and vishing are all references to the act of trying to steal someone's private or confidential data through the means of a scam email, text message or phone call.

  • Phishing - Normally in reference the scam emails
  • Smishing - Normally in reference to scam text messages
  • Vishing - Normally in reference to scam phone calls

As you can see from the above, phishing can happen across many devices. That is why it can be tricky to catch.

In the workplace though, phishing mainly happens through emails.
