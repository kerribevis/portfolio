Hi, I'm Kerri.

I am an e-learning developer.

I became interested in education when I was studying my undergraduate degree. I completed modules in language learning and language acquisition, and this created in me a fascination with the process of human learning.

I wanted to find out what created a positive learning experience and what could negatively impact one.

I completed a BA in English Studies. I then completed an Honours year in Applied Language Studies. My research essay was an examination of the impact of colonial education on students' performance in university level academic literacy.

I worked in the copywriting industry and then moved to the edu-tech industry. This year, I decided to pursue an e-learning development certificate from Durham College.

During my HTML and CSS module, I heard the siren song of computer programming. I decided to pause my certificate and pursue a Diploma in Computer Programming from Algonquin College. I am currently studying towards that diploma.


Why computer programming and instructional design?

They may not be the most 'normal' combination but I wanted to be able to create learning experiences from scratch. I didn't want to be held back because I did not understand the backend of LMS platforms or websites.

I want to create captivating, beautiful and accessible learning experiences for all learners. To do this, I wanted to be able to understand not just the theory of instructional design but the technical side too.
